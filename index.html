An overview of effective altruism

beginnings

1971: The seeds were sown by Peter Singer's Famine, Affluence and Morality.

If it is in our power to prevent something bad from happening, without thereby sacrificing anything of comparable moral importance, we ought, morally, to do it.

1996: Living High and Letting Die, by Peter Unger, goes even further...

growth

"Effective altruism" came in to use around 2010.

Centre for Effective Altruism, Giving What We Can, The Life You Can Save, Bill Gates?

ideas

Not so extreme as Peter Singer's belief that you should give until you reach the same level as those you're giving to.

Being in the richest 5%, we have the opportunity (rather than an obligation) to give a significant portion of our income without reducing our quality of life.

Donations should go where they will have the biggest impact. Price for a guide dog in UK. Price for sightsaving treatment.

controversy

Most people give for the warm glow.

Not systemic changes / western saviour

Treasure what you can measure - what about things which are harder to measure, but potentially very effective?
